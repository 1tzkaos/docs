`pool_event`s are the non-swap things that happen to an AMM pool: someone adds liquidity, someone removes liquidity, or someone burns LP tokens. They live alongside swaps in the swap index and are exposed via [`GET /pool-events`](/api-reference) and as `pool_event`-typed frames on the [WebSocket](/streaming/websocket).

## Event types

| `event_type` | What happened |
| --- | --- |
| `lp_deposit` | Someone added liquidity to the pool. `base_amount` + `quote_amount` are what they put in; `lp_token_amount` is what they got back. |
| `lp_withdraw` | Someone removed liquidity. Same field meanings, signs reversed economically. |
| `lp_burn` | An SPL `Burn` against the LP mint. `base_amount` and `quote_amount` are 0 — only `lp_token_amount` is meaningful. This is the signal you look for to confirm a "LP burnt" rug-pull-resistance claim. |

The `dex` field tells you which AMM emitted the event: `pumpswap`, `raydium`, or `meteora`. Each DEX has its own LP-token math and its own event-decoding rules. Two consequences:

- **Amounts are pre-decimals.** `base_amount` and `quote_amount` are in base units. For SOL, divide by 1e9. For tokens, divide by 10**token_decimals (look up via [`GET /tokens/{mint}`](/api-reference)).
- **`lp_token_amount` on Meteora can saturate.** Meteora's DAMMv2 uses a 128-bit LP-token field internally. When the value exceeds `u64::MAX` we truncate to `2^64 - 1` rather than dropping the event. Treat the maximum value as "very large" rather than a literal amount. Pumpswap and Raydium fit in 64 bits and do not saturate.

## Paging

`GET /pool-events` requires `token` and pages newest-first. To get the next page, take `events[events.length-1].timestamp_ms` and pass it as `before`. `has_more` is true when the page filled to the requested `limit`.

## Live stream

The same shape arrives on `wss://ws.dexploit.dev/ws/swaps` as a `{"type":"pool_event", …}` frame. You don't subscribe to pool-events separately — any subscription that matches the pool or the token via [WebSocket filters](/streaming/websocket#supported-filters) receives both `swap` and `pool_event` frames. Discriminate on the top-level `type` field.
